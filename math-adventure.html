About

During the Winter 2016 quarter, my content strategy class worked with Math Adventure to help remodel their website to support their central business goals:

  • 1. Increasing the amount and type of Math Adventure events
  • 2. Creating a DIY program for out-of-area schools

This project contains research observations and recommendations for remodeling the Math Adventure website that will help with clarify messaging across channels to educate potential customers and users.


Content Inventory

Each student first created a content inventory of the Math Adventure website to understand the current state of the website. A Master inventory was eventually created from each individual inventory for the whole class to use when conducting a qualitative content audit later on in the quarter.

Competitive Audit

To conduct a competitive audit, I looked into the websites of four other similar math programs: My Eye Level Bellevue, Zeno Math, Girls Rock Math, and Launch Math. This audit allowed me to find opportunities for improvement, identify differentiators, and set a baseline for measuring improvements.


The scorecard I created was heavily inspired by Forrester's Web Site User Experience Review. Each program was given a score of -1, 0, or 1 on each of the 15 heuristics, resulting in a maximum score of 15. A score of -1 was given if the heuristic was not fulfilled, or fulfilled extremely poorly. A score of 0 was given if the heuristic was met moderately. Finally, a score of 1 was given if the heuristic was fulfilled well.

Content Audit

With the Master Inventory, a qualitative content audit was done to evaluate whether or not the content on the site was any good. 8 heuristics were created, and each one was given a score of "Yay," "Meh," and "Nay," which is similar to the scoring system used in the competitive audit.

Presentations

Our client visited the class to listen to a three-minute presentation from each student. I chose to focus on re-designing the content on the homepage to help with converting their audience into participants of Math Adventure events, and ultimately into supporters to help them expand.


The main problem I found with the website was the homepage: there was little amounts of immediate relevance to an audience who did not understand what Math Adventure was. As such, I proposed to add a sub-headline, additional key points to create a value proposition, and effective calls-to-action that will create forward paths.

Final Report

A final report was done to summarize my findings and to give recommendations, backed up by evidence, to the client.
